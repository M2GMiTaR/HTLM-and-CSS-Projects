Why Do Cats Sleep All Day

Cats sleep most of the day to conserve energy for hunting and other bursts of activity, a leftover instinct from their wild ancestors.
This behavior is also influenced by age, with kittens and senior cats typically sleeping more.
While some sleep is normal, a significant increase in sleeping could signal a health issue or emotional distress, in which case a veterinarian should be consulted.

Key Reasons for Sleeping

  • Energy Conservation: Cats are predators and require a lot of energy for hunting, even for their house-cat prey like toys.
    Sleeping allows them to "recharge" and store energy for these intense bursts of activity, which often occur at dawn and dusk.
  • Biological Instinct: Even though domestic cats are fed by humans, their bodies are still wired for the "hunt,
    feast, sleep, repeat" cycle of their ancestors, according to Purina UK.
  • Age: Kittens and senior cats tend to sleep more than adult cats due to growth and potential age-related health issues, respectively.
  • Health and Recovery: Sleep is crucial for muscle repair, digestion, and overall health. Cats may also sleep more to recover from injuries or relieve discomfort.
  • Stress and Anxiety:A new or stressed environment can cause a cat to sleep more as a way to decompress and cope.

Summary
